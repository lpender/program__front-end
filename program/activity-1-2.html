Front end development is the study of the languages, HTML, CSS and Javascript, which are are parsed and executed by your web browser each time you visit a web page.

If you're not already you should get very comfortable with the idea of "googling" that which you don't already know Google is a great tool for learning new things--the ability to study documentation, articles and tutorials is the bedrock of being a developer.

That said, things can often become overwhelming when synthesizing large amounts of information--a tutorial on the internet is, in all likelihood, not always a perfect fit to the actual problem at hand. In these situations, nothing replaces the value of an actual human being, guiding you and explaining multiple, overlapping concepts as they come up in the natural flow of completing a project. Your mentor has years of experience facing the same kinds of problems that you will be facing, and they will guide you through the toughest of times.

The intention of this course is not to prescriptively step you through each and every thing that you will need to learn these languages. For that type of experience, we recommend sites like CodeAcademy or W3Schools.

These sites are great, and we heartily recommend that you take the time to go through the tutorials they provide--but they won't fully prepare you for the experience of facing actual, on the job challenges.

This course is going to push you to your limits, and you will have to work hard, but take heart: your mentor is an expert with years of experience in the business. They will be there to guide you through the toughest times-- they are here to act not only as your mentor, but as your co-worker and, at times, your boss.

And lest the tone here become too intimidating, buck up! This is going to be fun! Solving problems and being creative are the meat and potatoes of coding, and, while they can be frustrating, there is little that can't be overcome with patience. The most important thing for a coder is not to give up. The rewards are great. Excellent job prospects for folks with front end skills aside, prepare to get used to the feeling of satsifaction that comes with overcoming obstacles, doing things that you weren't sure you could do.
